Explore Faisalabad's Landmark Gallery

Faisalabad, known as the Manchester of Pakistan, is a vibrant and historical city located in the heart of Punjab. It is renowned for its textile industry, historical landmarks, and beautiful landscapes. Faisalabad offers visitors a unique blend of modernity and tradition, with bustling bazaars, beautiful gardens, and iconic sites. The city is a must-visit for anyone interested in experiencing Pakistan's rich cultural heritage.

History of Faisalabad

Faisalabad was founded in 1898 as Lyallpur, named after Sir James Lyall, the Lieutenant Governor of the Punjab. It was developed as a market town to cater to the agricultural needs of the region. Over time, Faisalabad evolved into a major industrial hub, particularly in the textile industry, making it one of Pakistan's largest cities. Its historical significance, rich culture, and economic contributions continue to shape its identity today.
